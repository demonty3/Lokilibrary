## The headline question

v0.1's retro asked whether painted-3D + LLM-personalisation delivered the
magic the 2D prototype couldn't. v0.2 raises the parallel question: **does
the swap from the hard-coded 7-game library to the player's real library
amplify the personalisation feel, or does it expose how thin the manifest
output is?**

Concrete sub-questions worth answering honestly after walking it:

1. **The recognition beat.** Was there a moment of "oh, *that's* my Hades"?
   The Steam header on the recognition face has been there since v0.1 but
   the surrounding context (this archetype was picked for *me*, with role
   text Claude wrote for *my* game) is new.
2. **The metaphor coherence.** Did the organising metaphor feel like it
   actually reflected your behaviour, or did it read like generic
   slot-filling? If the latter, check the Stage 1 prompt preview (`<details>`
   in the connector panel) — is the SPEC §8 profile text actually
   capturing the right signal?
3. **The HLTB completion-fraction payoff.** Did "deeply lived-in" vs.
   "tried" vs. "engaged" descriptors land the right games on the right
   side? Civ VI at 6.4x main story should read very differently from
   Hollow Knight at 0.1x.
4. **The friction before the magic.** OpenID round-trip + cold-path
   library fetch + 15 achievements + 15 HLTB scrapes + Stage 1 LLM call =
   how long, and did it feel worth the wait? If yes, the 24h manifest
   cache makes second-visits instant. If the cold path was too long,
   consider parallelising more aggressively or caching at finer grain.

## Outstanding before this retro is meaningful

2. **Walk a friend through the loop.** v0.2's pitch is "your library
   becomes a world." The truest test is whether a non-developer who
   doesn't know what to expect has the right reaction.
